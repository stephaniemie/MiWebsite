The Problem

Poverty is a major problem in Puerto Rico. According to Puerto Rico Report, in 2020, the poverty rate in Puerto Rico stood at 43%, almost half of the island’s population. The rate of children living in poverty that same year stood at a shocking 57%. Factors such as limited job opportunities and natural disasters, which have caused many to lose their homes and jobs, have greatly contributed to these statistics.

How This Problem Affects The Community

  • Many people suffer from hunger and illnesses.
  • Crime rates can increase due to the lack of necessities in poor families or individuals, which affects everyone, no matter the social class.
  • Creates tension between social classes.
  • Creates a lack of opportunities for those less fortunate.
